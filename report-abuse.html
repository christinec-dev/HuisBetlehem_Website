Report Child Abuse

You have the power to report abuse and save a child's future.

If you suspect that a child is beimg abused, the SAPS website advises reporting suspected child abuse to the police, the Child Protection Unit, or a social worker at one of your local child and family welfare agencies. Severe abuse should be reported to Lifeline, Childline, or the Child Emergency Line.

Lifeline's website provides a list of Lifeline Centres in your area, as well as contact details for ChildLine .

LifeLine can be reached toll-free at: 0800 055 555.

The Child Emergency Line can be reached toll-free at: 0800 123 321

Child Welfare SA hotline for reporting abuse: 0861 4 CHILD (24453)

Abuse 101

Possible Signs of Child Abuse

Abuse can be physical, emotional or sexual, as well as them being neglected and deprived of basic needs like food or safety, usually by their parents and/or guardians.

Children who are abused usually:

Avoid any kind of touch or physical contact, or certain people.
Have a fear to go home & showing constant alert.
Has bruises, welts, burns or other injuries that can’t be explained.
Has Medical or Dental issues that go untreated.
Withdraw from friends and activitie
Pregnancy or STDs, especially for boys and girls under 14 years old.
Sexual knowledge that people usually have only when they’re older.
Running away from home or missing school.
Appearing dirty or efusing to change clothes in front of others.
Extreme behavior, such as being way too obedient or violent.
Poor weight gain, growth and eating habits.
Academic, speech, emotional, or learning problems.
