About Huis Betlehem

Our aim is to equip children with a safe environment to reach their potential.

With families falling apart because of poverty, economical uncertainties and unemployment, children are most often left vulnerable and traumatized. That's why there will always be a need for houses of safety for these children.

Established in 2000, the core nature of our business at Huis Betlehem is to help all damaged, abused, abandoned and neglected children who have been removed from their familial situations by Social Workers as prescribed by Children’s Act 805, 2005. We provide these children with a Place of Safety for a period of time until the court decides how these children must be handled (e.g. Foster care, place back to parents or in a children’s home).

We currently have two houses - one in White River and another one in Nelspruit which can host 6 to 8 children each under the supervision of a Social Worker, but with permanent live-in House Parents who provide an environment for these children to be loved and cared for.

We make sure that the well-being of the children is taken care of by qualified and registered social workers, and not solely by the employees or the Trustees of Huis Betlehem Trust. The feeding area (companies) for Huis Betlehem includes mainly the CMR, Childline, Child Welfare, and the Department of Social Welfare.

We Are Guardians

Our homes are a place of safety, where children feel loved.

Our dedicated houses in White River and Nelspruit continues to provide housing for children who were removed by Social Workers and the Childrens Court from situations where they were in danger, exploited or neglected.

During their stay at Huis Betlehem, we aim to give them a home that is as close to normal as it could be, whilst looking after the childs needs; whether it be emotional, physical, social, and educational.
