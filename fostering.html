Fostering 101

Why Become a Foster Parent?

In South Africa,thousands of children are in desperate need of loving care in a stable, family-like environment. Foster children have been removed from their families for a reason, and therefore they are in need of a household upbringing that is as normal as possible.

Sadly, thereâ€™s an exponential shortage of loving, secure homes that will unconditionally commit to making a difference in the lives of these children, and give them a second chance at life. As a Foster Parent you can offer these children a brighter future and the opportunity to achieve their full potential in life.

Where to Apply

If you want to look after a child who is in need of care and protection, apply to the Department of Social Development or an accredited child protection organisation for permission to foster the child.

Foster care is the temporary placement of a child who is in need of care and protection. The child is placed in the care of a suitable person who is not the parent or guardian of the child.

Foster children include those under 18 who have been abandoned or orphaned; be addicted to a dependence-producing substance; have been exploited or exposed to exploitation; be at risk if returned to the custody of the parent, guardian or care-giver; be in a state of physical or mental neglect, maltreated, abused or degraded by a parent, a care-giver, a person who has parental responsibilities.

Who qualifies to be a foster parent?

You can only foster a child for the period indicated in the court order. Once the indicated period of the court order has lapsed, the presiding officer may, after reviewing the recommendation from the social worker, extend the period of the court order.

To qualify as a foster parent, you must be:

18 years or older,
fit and proper,
willing and able to look after the child,
able to provide a favourable environment for the child's growth and development.
